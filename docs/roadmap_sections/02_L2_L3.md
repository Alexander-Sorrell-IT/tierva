## 1. The three decisions that drive every dollar here (make them explicit, then it's arithmetic)

The advisor's load-bearing note: the run-cost numbers are wrong-and-scary unless three architectural choices are
stated up front. They are:

### Decision A — the LLM inference price is an **ESTIMATE** (it is *not* in RESEARCH)
RESEARCH's "apply the unit costs, don't re-look-them-up" rule scopes to **§1 (data licensing) and §5 (tile/serving)**.
§3 is *wrap-don't-train guidance* and carries **no $/token figure.** L2's dominant marginal cost — and L3's
explanation cost — is **inference tokens**, which the docs do not price. So this doc carries a named-model planning
**ESTIMATE** (the same register FUNDRAISING/PAID_DATA use for un-quoted numbers), not a re-research.

> **Inference cost basis (ESTIMATE, 2026-06-04, planning-frame — re-quote at build time):** anchor to a *cheap/fast*
> model class (Haiku / Flash / small-Gemini tier), ESTIMATE **≈ $0.50 / 1M input tok, ≈ $2.50 / 1M output tok**
> blended **≈ $1 / 1M tokens** at a typical explainer's input-heavy mix. The **cascade** below — tokens/query →
> queries/user/mo → ×users → ÷cache-hit-rate — is the defensible, load-bearing part; the unit price is a tunable
> input. Premium L3 narration may route to a mid model (Sonnet-tier, ~10× the cheap tier) for the few high-value
> "what to grow and why" calls; default everything else to the cheap tier.

### Decision B — **scale cost is a caching story** (extend GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §4.2, don't re-derive)
The single most important correction to avoid a six-figure 1M-user number:

- **GAEZ suitability per cell is STATIC** — climate projections don't move between releases. Compute the L3 verdict
  for a cell **once**, cache it, serve it forever. This is *exactly* the gridded `(cell × month × variable)` cache
  GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §4.2 stands up for climatology, and the COG-point-sample fast path §2 Fix A — **reuse both.**
- **The L2 explainer for a given `(cell, season, data-state)` is largely cacheable too.** Two parcels in the same
  H3 cell, same month, same anomaly band get the **same explanation** — generate once, serve many. Only a fresh
  data-state (a new anomaly crossing a band) needs a fresh LLM call.
- **Therefore the honest marginal cost at 1M users = (cache hits ≈ $0) + (a small fraction of fresh calls).** If a
  number in this doc is costed as `users × full LLM+GAEZ compute`, it is **wrong** (per-user instead of per-unique-query).
  The per-request-vs-per-cell-precompute choice **is** the answer to "monthly run $ at 1M."

### Decision C — GAEZ hosting scope: ship the **curated subset**, note the full archive as a scale line
RESEARCH §2's product reading is "default **SSP3-7.0**, baseline vs **2041–2060** ('20 yrs out'), top-N crops,
rainfed vs irrigated." That is a **curated subset**, not the full multi-SSP × 4-window × 70-crop archive:

- **Curated (the shipping product):** ~top-20 crops × 2 windows (baseline + 2041-60) × 1 SSP × {rainfed, irrigated}
  ≈ **~80 Int16 GeoTIFF layers**. Storage ESTIMATE: GAEZ v5 ~1 km global Int16 ≈ low-hundreds-of-MB/layer compressed
  → **~tens of GB total → single-digit $/mo on R2/S3.** Point-sample it per cell exactly like L1's COG fast path;
  cache the per-cell verdict (Decision B).
- **Full archive (scale option, note only):** all SSPs × 4 future windows × 70+ crops = **multi-TB → ~$100/mo
  storage** (RESEARCH §5 object-storage rates). Cost it as an *option*, not the MVP. GAEZ **v4 also has a live
  ArcGIS REST point-query API** (RESEARCH §2) — zero-storage fallback for the coarse first cut while we host the
  v5 subset.
- **Resolution honesty (GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §5, non-negotiable):** GAEZ at ~1 km is **"your area," not "your land,"**
  until the 10 m downscale/fusion exists. The downscale is the moat work (and the only thing that earns the
  "your land" label for L3) — costed separately below as the premium, seed-funded slice.

**Shared assumptions (so the arithmetic is reproducible):**
- **$/dev-week = ESTIMATE $4–6k/dev-week** loaded (solo/AI-augmented contractor rate; Alex builds AI-only). One-time
  $ below = `dev-weeks × this`. Mark all as ESTIMATE.
- **Two archetypes only** (RESEARCH §3 wrap-don't-train): **(i) a geospatial data engineer** (COG/STAC, GeoTIFF
  point-sampling, downscaling) and **(ii) an LLM/RAG app engineer** (Farmer.Chat-style advisor). **No ML
  researcher, no climate scientist** — anything heavier contradicts RESEARCH §3.
- **Engagement (ESTIMATE):** L2 = a few queries/engaged-user/mo (it's diagnostic, checked seasonally), call it
  **~3 LLM-backed queries/user/mo**; only a fraction of users are engaged (assume **~20% MAU→engaged**). L3 = a
  premium subscriber cohort, far smaller than total users.

## 2. L2 — UNDERSTAND (the AI explainer)

**What it is (VISION §4):** *"What does it mean, and what's coming this season?"* An AI layer that **interprets** the
L1 readout, adds **40-yr Landsat historical context**, and a **near-term/seasonal forecast** ("soil drying, rain
unlikely 3 weeks, 2 droughts in your last 5 years"). Diagnostic + forward-looking *for the current season* — it does
**not** prescribe crops (that line is L3).

### SCOPE (what gets built)
1. **The explainer service** — wrap an LLM (Decision A) as a structured interpreter over the existing `/api/parcel`
   readout + `climatology.zscore_anomaly` output. **Reuse the BUILD_PLAN §1d seed**: keep the monthly z-score strip
   layout + trigger/corroborator roles; replace the *static notes* with AI-generated plain language. A Farmer.Chat-style
   RAG pattern (RESEARCH §3) grounds the model on the parcel's own numbers so it narrates facts, not hallucinations.
2. **40-yr history layer** — pull/cache Landsat (30 m, $0 data, Earthdata token held) so "2 droughts in your last 5
   years / greenest in a decade" is real. This is a *new ingest source* + cache, same pattern as the existing loaders.
3. **Seasonal/near-term forecast** — wrap open weather/seasonal signals (GraphCast/GenCast open weights; the
   ENSO scalar the BUILD_PLAN routes to L2; Copernicus EDO/GDO drought indices — all RESEARCH §3). Narrate, don't model.
4. **Conversational wrapper (optional, deferrable)** — a Farmer.Chat-style multilingual chat front; deferrable to a
   later sprint (the structured explainer ships value first).

### EFFORT
- **Geospatial data eng:** ~2 dev-weeks (Landsat history loader + cache; wire forecast signals — same loader archetype).
- **LLM/RAG app eng:** ~3–4 dev-weeks (the interpreter prompt + RAG grounding on the readout, the strip→narrative
  bridge, the cache key in Decision B, eval harness against the existing TEST_PLAN style).
- **Total ≈ 5–6 dev-weeks**, **two archetypes**, no researcher. *Lower than greenfield because the explainer seed +
  anomaly engine + $0 history already exist.* (ESTIMATE)

### ONE-TIME BUILD $
**≈ $25–35k** (ESTIMATE = 5–6 dev-weeks × $4–6k). Negligible non-labor: $0 data (Landsat/forecast all free), trivial
dev-time inference spend.

### MONTHLY RUN $ (the cascade — Decision A × Decision B)
Per-query token cost ESTIMATE: a grounded explainer call ≈ **~2k input (readout + history + prompt) + ~0.5k output ≈
2.5k tok/query → ≈ $0.0025/uncached query** at the cheap-tier blended rate. Then:

| Scale (total users) | Engaged MAU (~20%) | Fresh (uncached) LLM queries/mo* | LLM $/mo (ESTIMATE) | + Landsat history cache/host | **L2 total /mo (ESTIMATE)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1k** | ~200 | ~600 (low cache early) | **< $5** | ~$1 (R2, tens of GB tiles cached) | **~$5–10** |
| **100k** | ~20k | ~60k × 3, **~70% cache hit** → ~18k fresh | **~$45** | ~$10–20 | **~$60–90** |
| **1M** | ~200k | 600k × 3, **~85% cache hit** → ~90k fresh | **~$225** | ~$30–50 | **~$300–500** |

\* *Cache hit rate climbs with scale because more users fall into already-explained `(cell, season, data-state)`
buckets (Decision B). **This is why the 1M number is low-hundreds, not tens-of-thousands.** Costed per-user
(no cache), 1M would be ~$15k/mo — the wrong, scary number the advisor warned against.*

**Sanity:** matches the advisor's band (uncached ≈ tens of $k/mo at 1M; **cached ≈ low-single-digit $k or less**).

### DEPENDENCIES
- **L1's `/api/parcel` readout + `climatology` anomaly engine** (DONE — M0 first slice). L2 narrates their output.
- **GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §4.2 gridded cache** to make the explanation cache key real at scale (M1 work).
- Earthdata token for Landsat (held).

### UNLOCKS
- Turns L1's raw numbers into a *retained* product (the reason a user comes back each season).
- **Is the on-ramp to L3** — once a user trusts "what it means," "what should I grow" (the paid tier) is the natural
  next click.
- Powers the **donor-facing transparency narration** ("here's the data that will fire the payout") for the Pacto
  giving layer (VISION §4 giving layer; RESEARCH §6) — same explainer, donor framing.

### CAPITAL TRANCHE
**Mostly the non-dilutive / 2048 Fast-Track lane** ($150–750k grants to the $600k floor; or a $250–750k Fast-Track —
FUNDRAISING §4, EQUITY). L2 is **low-regret integration** that can start pre-proof-unlock like Phase 0 (it needs no
real-money rail). Vocabulary split (BUILD_PLAN §4 #6): the **giving-narration** framing → grant room; the
**"AI Earth-intelligence" data-platform** framing → the 2048 room.

## 3. L3 — THE GARDENER (the premium 20-yr crop-suitability decision layer)

**What it is (VISION §4):** *"What's the best thing to grow on my exact land — and why?"* A ranked crop recommendation
**with the reasoning**, future-proofed by the **20-yr climate simulation bundled with the prescription** (the
simulation alone = anxiety; bundled with "so do this" = value). **The moat is the FUSION + the consumer plain-language
layer, NOT the climate science** — FAO already did the science (RESEARCH §2). **L3 wraps GAEZ; it does not build models.**

### SCOPE — split into the cheap-core engine and the premium-fusion moat
**3a. Cheap core — wrap GAEZ v5 (the engine):**
- Host the **curated GAEZ subset** (Decision C: ~80 layers, ~tens of GB) on R2/S3; point-sample per `(lat,lon)` with
  the L1 COG fast path (GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §2 Fix A). Use the **GAEZ v4 ArcGIS REST point-query** (RESEARCH §2) as the
  zero-storage coarse first cut.
- **The L3 algorithm (RESEARCH §2, verbatim spec — do not re-derive):** sample the GAEZ index for every crop, baseline
  vs the 2041–2060 window, default SSP3-7.0 (one number; optimistic/worst as toggles) → rank, compute Δ → message:
  rising-suitability = "**start growing X**"; current crop falling below threshold = "**Y becoming unsuitable — switch
  by year N**."
- Plain-language it with the **L2 explainer** (reused, premium prompt) → the "✓ sun ✓ drainage ✓ your rainfall; avoid
  maíz — too dry by July" output.

**3b. Premium-fusion moat (the "your land" 10 m answer — seed-funded):**
- **Downscale/fuse** GAEZ (~1 km) + NEX-GDDP trajectory (~25 km context) + **AlphaEarth 10 m embeddings** + the
  parcel's **real satellite history** (from L1/L2) into one **10 m, per-farm, plain-language verdict** (RESEARCH §2/§3).
  *Each ingredient is free; **no product assembles them for a non-expert** — this is the moat (RESEARCH §3/§4 gap #3).*
- **Resolution honesty (Decision C / GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §5):** GAEZ-only = labeled **"your area."** Only after the 10 m
  downscale may L3 say **"your land."** The downscale is what converts the honest-but-coarse free answer into the
  premium parcel verdict — and it's where the **paid parcel close-up bundles in** (PAID_DATA §4: sub-meter Satellogic
  is bundled into the L3 subscription, the one tier where "look at MY exact land" pays for itself).

### EFFORT
- **3a cheap core:** geospatial eng ~3 dev-weeks (curated GAEZ ingest + point-sample + per-cell cache) + LLM eng ~1–2
  dev-weeks (the ranking→prescription prompt, reusing L2). **≈ 4–5 dev-weeks.**
- **3b premium fusion:** geospatial eng ~4–6 dev-weeks (the downscale/fusion pipeline — AlphaEarth embeddings +
  GAEZ + history; this is the hard, moat part) + ~1–2 weeks integration/eval. **≈ 6–8 dev-weeks.** Still
  *engineering*, not research (AlphaEarth/Prithvi are pretrained, wrap them — RESEARCH §3). (ESTIMATE)
- **Total L3 ≈ 10–13 dev-weeks** across the two archetypes; no climate scientist.

### ONE-TIME BUILD $
- **3a cheap core ≈ $20–30k** (4–5 dev-weeks × $4–6k).
- **3b premium fusion ≈ $30–50k** (6–8 dev-weeks × $4–6k).
- **Total ≈ $50–80k** (ESTIMATE). Plus near-zero one-time data cost (GAEZ CC-BY free; AlphaEarth in Earth Engine —
  *note the GEE commercial caveat, RESEARCH §1B: GEE free tier is noncommercial → either export embeddings in a
  one-off batch composite, or budget the GEE commercial $2,000/mo if used live; designed as a one-off export to avoid
  the monthly fee*).

### MONTHLY RUN $ (GAEZ host + downscale compute + premium narration — Decisions B & C)
GAEZ verdicts are **static per cell** → compute once, cache forever (Decision B). So run cost = hosting + a thin
trickle of fresh downscale/narration for newly-touched cells, **not** per-user model runs.

| Scale | GAEZ curated host (Decision C) | Downscale compute (per **new cell**, cached) | Premium narration (mid-tier LLM, subscribers only) | **L3 total /mo (ESTIMATE)** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1k users** | ~$1–5 (tens of GB, R2) | ~$5 (few cells warmed) | < $5 (small paid cohort) | **~$10–20** |
| **100k users** | ~$5–10 | ~$20–50 (region cells warmed once) | ~$30–60 | **~$60–120** |
| **1M users** | ~$5–10 (curated) **or ~$100 if full archive (Decision C option)** | ~$50–150 (more cells; amortized once-each) | ~$150–300 | **~$250–500 (curated) / ~$350–600 (full archive)** |

**Why so low at 1M:** the GAEZ answer for a cell is computed/downscaled **once** and served to every user in it —
the §4.2 cache pattern. Costed per-user (re-downscaling per request) the 1M number would be five-to-six figures —
again the wrong, scary number. The honest cost is *hosting + a one-time warm per cell*.

### DEPENDENCIES
- **Both L3 sub-layers ride on L1's parcel readout + climatology** (DONE) and **L2's explainer** (built in §2 — reuse
  the prompt/RAG infra). 3b additionally needs the **GLOBAL_ROLLOUT §4.2 gridded cache** (to store per-cell verdicts)
  and the **§2 Fix A point-sample path** (to read GAEZ COGs cheaply).
- 3b's "your land" claim depends on the **paid parcel close-up adapter** (PAID_DATA §5 Satellogic) for the bundled
  sub-meter look — but **never on the on-chain trigger path** (PAID_DATA §3 RED LINE: paid imagery is
  consumer/diagnostic only).

### UNLOCKS
- **L3 is the revenue tier that funds everything** (VISION §4). It is the clearest moat in the competitive map
  (RESEARCH §4 gap #3: *nobody* offers forward-looking, per-individual, 20-yr crop advice). **Unit economics:** marginal
  cost/query is **cents** (cache hit + a thin narration), against a comp price point of **Farmonaut $10–200/mo**
  (RESEARCH §4) — i.e. a subscriber covers the layer's *entire monthly run cost* many times over. That gross margin is
  what "funds everything" means concretely: L3 subscription revenue subsidizes the free L1 spine + the giving rail.
- Enables the **premium paid-imagery bundle** (PAID_DATA §4) — the one place sub-meter pays for itself.

### CAPITAL TRANCHE
- **3a cheap core (wrap GAEZ) = non-dilutive / Fast-Track lane** — low-regret integration, can start pre-unlock.
- **3b premium fusion + the conversational advisor at scale = SEED, post-proof-unlock, the 2048 framing.** This is the
  **commercial moat / data-platform** story — exactly the "Earth-intelligence data platform" vocabulary that belongs in
  the 2048 room (BUILD_PLAN §4 #6), and the revenue tier that justifies the $1–3M seed at a $10–13M post (EQUITY).
  Sequencing-is-dilution (EQUITY): build 3a on cheap grant money to *raise the proof/traction*, then sell equity for
  3b at the higher, evidence-backed cap.
