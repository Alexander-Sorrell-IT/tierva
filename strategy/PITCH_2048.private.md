## The one-liner

> **A deep-tech Earth-intelligence data platform — a system of record for what's true about any piece of land —
> with a compounding, defensible data moat, proven live on a public chain on real 2023 Honduras drought data.**

## Why now / why generational (their weakest-covered test)

Two curves just crossed. **(1)** The whole planet is now sensed continuously, for free, at 10 m
(Copernicus/NASA) — the raw truth about every parcel on Earth is a commodity input. **(2)** Decision systems that
*act* on that truth still wait on committees, adjusters, and reports. The generational company is the **execution
and intelligence layer** that closes that gap: turn raw EO into a per-parcel answer (*what is true → what it
means → what to do*), then let that answer **settle outcomes automatically and auditably.** Earth-intelligence is
the category; agricultural land is vertical #1, not the ceiling.

## The moat — where it actually lives (lead here, not on the satellites)

Open EO is a *commodity input* and **fails their proprietary-data test outright** — so we never claim it as the
moat. The moat **compounds with use**:

1. **Fused, proprietary data** — open EO + ground-truth + accumulated **outcome/disbursement data** (what fired,
   what paid, what verified). Every event makes the next prediction better. *This* is the system of record.
2. **The models** — per-parcel downscaling + crop-suitability fusion (FAO GAEZ + NEX-GDDP + 10 m embeddings) into
   one plain-language answer no expert portal delivers.
3. **The validator / partner network** — the institutions, ground agents, and rails that make an answer
   *actionable and trusted*. Network effects, not a dataset.
4. **The settlement rails** — auditable, automatic outcome execution. Defensible *integration*, commodity inputs.

## Product — the system of record, three jobs (commercial framing)

| Tier | The job | Commercial role |
|---|---|---|
| **L1 — SEE** | the truth about any parcel, now | top-of-funnel / data primitive; mass free reach; **mostly built** (15 loaders + map) |
| **L2 — UNDERSTAND** | what it means + near-term forecast | the affordable conversion tier; AI interpretation + 40-yr history |
| **L3 — DECIDE ("the gardener")** | what to do on this exact parcel, future-proofed 20 yrs | **premium** — funds the platform; per-parcel suitability + climate trajectory |

Settlement rails ride underneath all three: when the data confirms a condition, the contract executes the outcome
automatically — **the customer watches the exact data that fires it.** That auditability is the product, not the
chain.

## Business model

- **Free spine = $0 data cost** — L1 runs entirely on free-for-commercial Copernicus/NASA; cost is compute/host,
  not licensing (R2 + TiTiler object storage, 20–300× cheaper than Mapbox/GEE).
- **Premium upsell where the free tier breaks:** ① **parcel resolution** — 10 m can't resolve a smallholder's
  1-acre plot; charge for the close-up (Satellogic ~$4.50/km²). ② **all-weather SAR** for trigger confirmation.
- **L3 "gardener"** = the premium decision layer that funds everything.

## The ask — and **founder control is non-negotiable**

> **Hard constraint: Alex keeps control of the company.** The raise is structured so Alex remains controlling
> CEO with board control through seed. We sell *equity*, not *the company*.

How that's enforced, concretely:
- **Standard post-money SAFE (YC template) — no board seat, no protective control terms at this stage.** A SAFE
  defers governance to the priced round; first money in does **not** get control.
- **Grants first to the $600K floor (0% dilution)** — covers runway for free and *raises* the price of later
  equity. Keeps us 2048 Fast-Track-eligible (<$2M raised).
- **Right-size the 2048 check to protect control:** Fast-Track **$250–750K now** (fits the floor, low dilution),
  OR the **$2M lead only at the proof-unlock** (≈$10–13M post → 15–20% to them, **~80%+ kept**). Avoid the trap:
  $2M now at a pre-proof $4M = handing over half. Don't.
- **Co-founder equity comes from the founder pool, before investors** — Alex's controlling stake set *first*.
- **Sell into the Delaware C-corp only.** The NM LLC (infra/hardware CapEx) is asset-financed separately and
  stays **out** of the equity round, so it never dilutes the operating company.
- **Track cumulative dilution** (two 20% rounds = 64% kept, not 60%) — keep founder ownership + board control
  healthy across the *whole* sequence, not one round.
