# PART I — THE GLOBAL IDEA (at full scale)

## 1. What it actually is — a coordination kernel, not a fintech product
"Parametric public finance" is **rung one** — the fundable framing, not the idea. The real thing is a **domain-agnostic kernel for autonomous coordination**:

> **Reality → open, universal, recomputable sensing → multi-source consensus (no trusted party) → self-executing code → an outcome anyone can re-verify.**

Strip "drought" and "cash" out and what remains is a primitive for **governing any shared resource or public decision by code that reads the world directly** — deleting the human bureaucratic bottleneck between *knowing* and *acting*. It is the **missing execution layer** between "we can now sense the whole planet for free" and "systems still wait on committees to move."

## 2. At its limit — the execution layer of a self-governing system
Scaled to its ceiling, the kernel becomes the **coordination/execution layer at civilization scale**: relief, allocation, infrastructure, logistics, energy — all responding autonomously and *verifiably* to real conditions. **No single point of failure. Replicable anywhere. Owned by no one. Auditable by everyone.** One substrate, every part multi-use, clean-sheet — the method applied to governance itself.

## 3. The proven kernel — what the contest upgraded it with
The hackathon didn't build a drought app. On the **hardest possible test case** (public money, real lives, real 2023 data, adversarial scrutiny) it **validated every layer of the kernel end-to-end**:

| Kernel layer | Proven by Pacto Seco | Principle |
|---|---|---|
| **Sensing** — free, universal, recomputable | Copernicus consensus index, no auth, no vendor | one substrate; inputs available everywhere |
| **Consensus / integrity** — no trusted party | ≥2 independent sensors must agree; killed the Jan false-fire | **no single point of failure**, designed in |
| **Autonomous execution** — no human in loop | contract disburses itself when consensus holds 20 days | **delete the inherited step** |
| **Audit / legitimacy** — trust without a trustee | every input recomputable from public IDs; no commercial oracle | falsifiable by anyone |
| **Replicable archetype** — not a one-off | same engine → any trigger / region / payout | the substrate composes |

**Before the contest:** "wouldn't it be powerful if public systems executed themselves on open data." **After:** a working, adversarially-verified instance you can point at — *"the kernel works; here it is on real money and real satellites; everything above it is the same primitive scaled."* That is the difference between a manifesto and a beachhead.

## 4. Naming & architecture
**`Pacto`** = the umbrella protocol (the kernel). **`Pacto Seco`** ("dry pact") = drought vertical #1 / proof-of-kernel. The family extends cleanly: Pacto + flood, frost, heat, price-collapse, displacement — same consensus-and-execute engine, different sensors and payouts.

## 5. The discipline — vision expansive, ask narrow
The vision is the kernel at civilization scale. **The *ask* is always a wedge:** LAC drought, IDB pilot as the first real deployment. Investors and funders back a wedge with a credible first deployment; the global category is the *upside*, not the pitch. Conflating the two is what kills raises.

# PART II — THE RAISE (my investor strategy)

## 6. The one principle that resolves "how much"
**Seed checks are priced on *proof at the moment*, not on vision — and you use a different instrument for each asset class.** The "$1–2M ceiling" bounds *one* instrument (an equity seed against the insurtech comp set). It does **not** bound you, the idea, or your total capital. Your big capital comes through a *different* instrument with no such ceiling (Track B below). So: **don't push the big number through the small-ceilinged instrument.**

## 7. Two tracks of capital — never merge them
| | **Track A — the project / pilot** | **Track B — the infrastructure** |
|---|---|---|
| **What** | Pacto Seco software, the kernel, the pilot, ops, founder salary | Land + data centers (the $3M+ CapEx) |
| **Money** | **Grants now → SAFE later** | **Infrastructure / asset finance** (debt or strategic, against the asset) |
| **Ceiling** | Small (insurtech comps): $150–750K now, $1–2M seed | **No insurtech ceiling** — priced on the asset, not pilot traction |
| **Home** | **Delaware Inc (C-corp)** | **New Mexico LLC** |
| **Link** | runs *on* Track B's compute | leases compute to Track A; C-corp is its **anchor tenant** |

Merging them sinks both: a funder sees "land + data center" inside a drought-pilot ask and the pilot loses credibility; an infra lender sees a pre-revenue pilot and won't lend against it. Split, both fund.

## 8. Your two entities, mapped (you already have what this needs)
- **Delaware Inc (C-corp)** → **operating co**: holds the project IP, receives the **grants + the SAFE/seed**. Standard post-money SAFE docs, **QSBS-eligible**, what investors + IDB expect. *Checks: confirm it's clean/single-purpose with this IP assigned; if it carries an S-election, revoke it before raising.*

No NewCo needed. This is the structure investors *and* infra lenders both want.

## 12. Regulatory architecture — the one to get exactly right
The danger is **not** a token (have none; keep it that way). It's **custodying/disbursing beneficiary funds**, which can make you a money transmitter / VASP.
- **The shielding design (also the right framing):** Pacto Seco is the **trigger / consensus-oracle / accounting layer** — it decides *when* and *how much*, on-chain, auditably. The **actual fiat to households is moved by a licensed local payer / mobile-money agent / government treasury.** Then you are not the custodian → sidestep money-transmitter status. "A self-executing line of a budget, **not a wallet**."
- **Honduras (decisive):** CNBS Resolution 003/2024 prohibits supervised financial institutions from holding/operating crypto — so "stablecoin straight into Honduran bank accounts" is dead; route through a licensed payer.
- **US:** GENIUS Act + 2026 FinCEN/OFAC proposals pull stablecoin flows under the BSA — don't rely on a "disbursing our own funds" exemption.
- **Action:** US-fintech + Honduran counsel opinion letter **before any real-money pilot** (not before the demo).

"2-sensor consensus proven on real 2023 data; 22-test contract proven locally on anvil; nothing yet live on a public chain; no real disbursement; IDB is a channel we're walking, not a deal we've closed." Hype gets discounted in diligence; verifiable candor is the differentiator with the Web3 public-goods crowd and the credibility anchor with IDB. Keep it **verbatim** in every funder touch.
